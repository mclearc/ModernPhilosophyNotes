Hume on Causation

Class notes (4/10) – do not cite or circulate

Sections 4-7 of the Enquiry present Hume’s treatment of the question as to how we come to have beliefs concerning unobserved or future entities or events, based merely on our current or remembered experience. Hume comes to rather famous (or infamous) skeptical conclusions concerning the possibility of knowledge of such things, and his argument became the basis for what has come to be know as the problem of induction.
